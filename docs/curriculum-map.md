# Curriculum Map — *Français pour bébas* (12 modules)

Designed for one fluent Spanish+English speaker → Nice. Governed by
[`teaching-charter.md`](teaching-charter.md). Every module: **grammar first, taught
on cognate vocabulary** (load lands on the new structure), **then expanded to
vocabulary**; **every example is a full sentence with audio**; flashcards + grammar
quizzes throughout. Past tenses, polite conditional, and (sparingly) the subjunctive
appear **from the very beginning**, not deferred. Target ≈ 1000 words across the arc.

| # | Module | Grammar spine | Cognate leverage | New vocab (~) |
|---|--------|---------------|------------------|:---:|
| 1 | **Être** — to be | all tenses of être (présent focus); c'est, on | cognate adjectives (important, possible, différent…) | 90 |
| 2 | **Avoir** — to have | avoir all tenses; il y a | travel cognate nouns (réservation, pharmacie, menu) | 85 |
| 3 | **-er verbs** | the big regular class, présent; polite je voudrais/j'aimerais | -ar→-er cognate verbs (préparer, visiter, réserver) | 95 |
| 4 | **Le passé composé** | completed events, aux. avoir + participle | -é participles from cognate verbs; anchor = pretérito | 85 |
| 5 | **L'imparfait** | background/habit/state; **PC vs imparfait** contrast | anchor = imperfecto (she already feels the aspect) | 80 |
| 6 | **Irregular verbs + futur proche** | aller, faire, venir, pouvoir, vouloir, devoir; je vais + inf; PC with être (agreement) | high-frequency verbs, movement | 90 |
| 7 | **Questions, negation, connectors** | est-ce que, qu'est-ce que, où/quand/combien; ne…pas (+ dropped ne); et/mais/parce que/donc | the non-cognate **core glue** (~100) | 100 |
| 8 | **Gender, articles & agreement** | le/la/les, un/une/des, du/de la; adjective placement + agreement; mon/ma/mes | cognate nouns sorted by gender | 85 |
| 9 | **Pronouns & daily routine** | object pronouns (le/la/lui/y/en); reflexives (se laver…); on | pronominal verbs, body/routine | 85 |
| 10 | **The living subjunctive** | present subj as chunks (il faut que, je veux que, avant que, pour que, bien que); **the Spanish tense-agreement trap** | emotion/necessity cognate triggers | 70 |
| 11 | **Conditional & politeness** | je voudrais/j'aimerais/pourrais; si + imparfait → conditionnel; passé du subjonctif; **quisiera → voudrais** | polite request frames | 70 |
| 12 | **Nice & survival (capstone)** | register consolidation (tu/vous), everything in use | directions, café, shopping, transport, numbers/time/money, emergencies | 95 |

**Cross-cutting drill tracks (every module feeds them):**
- **Flashcards** — Spanish→French recall, 3 hard : 2 cognate mix, spaced repetition.
- **Grammar quizzes** — conjugation fill-in, tense choice (PC vs imparfait), subjunctive-trigger recognition, es→fr translation, and the **Spanish-trap** category (grammar faux-amis).
- **Ear training** — every item is a spoken sentence; listen-and-choose.

**Sequencing principle:** grammar concepts arrive early and get *reinforced* across
later modules with widening vocabulary — she should be able to make sense of common
phrases *immediately*, because she already owns two closely related languages.

**Build order:** M1–M2 exist (upgraded to the sentence-audio + all-tenses standard).
M3–M12 authored in bulk against this map + the exemplar module structure.
